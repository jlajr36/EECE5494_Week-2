**MG Week 2: Assignment 2.1: Problem Domain**
**Black Box: Home Weather Station**
**March 19, 2026**

**1. Raspberry Pi Weather Station**
Your system of interest is a **home weather station**. Use the following site as a reference for your weather station: [https://projects.raspberrypi.org/en/projects/build-your-own-weather-station/](https://projects.raspberrypi.org/en/projects/build-your-own-weather-station/). You are free to enhance and modify your weather station, but it **must satisfy the core purpose** described by the Raspberry Pi site. For example, you can add an additional sensor, swap one sensor type for another, or use a different communication technology.

In addition to the hardware and embedded software, your system must also include **weather monitoring software** to run on a device of your choice (e.g., smartphone, laptop, etc.). For **Measures of Effectiveness (MoEs)**, include communication range (WiFi) and weather reading archiving.

**2. Problem Domain Black Box**
**Black-Box Perspective**
Problem domain analysis and definition from the black-box perspective is the first step towards specifying what the System of Interest (SoI) shall do to fulfill the stakeholders’ expectations. The goal of this phase is to **understand how the SoI interacts with its environment** to solve the given challenges. As the SoI is considered a black box, the analysis is **not concerned with its internal structure and behavior**. The analysis is performed to define the main inputs and outputs, black-box functions, and quantifiable characteristics of the SoI, while operating in a variety of system contexts.

Use the **MagicGrid BOK** (Book of Knowledge) as a reference.

### **Notes of Understanding**

*   **Core Objective:** The goal of this assignment is to perform the initial "Problem Domain" analysis for a home weather station using a **Black-Box perspective**. This means you focus on **what the system does** (inputs, outputs, and external interactions) rather than **how it works internally**.
*   **System Components:** The project requires a trifecta of components: the **physical hardware** (Raspberry Pi and sensors), the **embedded software** to run that hardware, and an **external monitoring application** for a smartphone or computer.
*   **Methodology:** The project strictly follows the **MagicGrid framework**. You are expected to use Model-Based Systems Engineering (MBSE) principles to define stakeholder needs, use cases, and system context.
*   **Key Performance Indicators:** You must specifically define and model **Measures of Effectiveness (MoEs)**, with a required focus on **WiFi communication range** and the **archiving of weather data**.
*   **Modeling Deliverables:** Success is measured by the creation of specific SysML (Systems Modeling Language) artifacts, including **BDDs, IBDs, Use Case diagrams, and Activity Diagrams**. These must be captured within a Teamwork Cloud (TWC) project.
